About Me

I am an incoming PhD student in Informatics and Computing at Northern Arizona University, where I will be working under the guidance of Dr. Joseph Mihaljevic. My research in computational epidemiology links mathematical models to spatiotemporal surveillance data to track disease patterns. This includes developing algorithms, creating modeling software, and using machine learning to identify factors driving disease spread. These methods apply to various pathogens, improving our understanding and response to infectious diseases.

Previously, I spent two years of my PhD at Kennesaw State University, where I concentrated on Large Language Models in the medical domain. I hold a Bachelor’s degree in Computer Science from Brac University, Bangladesh, where I also completed my school and college education.
